📖 About Amber Fort

Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is a magnificent hilltop fortress located just 11 km from Jaipur. Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, it stands as one of the finest examples of Rajput military architecture blended with Mughal and Hindu styles.

The fort is constructed using pale yellow and pink sandstone and white marble. Its grand structure overlooks the Maota Lake, offering breathtaking reflections at sunrise. The complex consists of four sections — each with its own courtyard — including the grand Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), the ornate Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), and the Sukh Niwas with its unique air-cooling water channels.

In 2013, Amber Fort was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site as part of the Hill Forts of Rajasthan group. Today, it remains one of the most visited monuments in India.

🏛️ Historical Significance

Amber was the capital of the Kachwaha Rajput clan for several centuries before Jaipur was founded. The fort served as the royal residence of the Rajput Maharajas until the 18th century. Its strategic hilltop location made it an impregnable fortress, and its grand interiors reflected the immense wealth and artistic sophistication of its rulers.

Emperor Akbar was a frequent visitor, and the alliance between the Kachwahas and the Mughal Empire was symbolized in the fort's unique architectural style — a harmonious fusion of two great traditions.
